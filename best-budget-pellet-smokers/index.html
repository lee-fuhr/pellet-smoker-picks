Quick answer

The Green Mountain Grill Daniel Boone is the best budget pellet smoker overall, offering the most consistent temperature control and robust build for its price.

If you want a larger hopper and easier ash cleanup, get the Camp Chef SmokePro DLX; if you need the cheapest option that still gets the job done, go with the Z Grills 700D.

Pellet smokers have made real wood-fired barbecue accessible to anyone with a power outlet. You load the hopper, set the temperature, and let the auger and fan do the work, no tending coals, no wrestling with offsets. For home cooks who want great flavor without a second career in fire management, a pellet grill is the obvious choice. The trick is finding one that holds temperature well, cooks evenly, and won’t fall apart after a season of use, all without spending a fortune. When shopping on a budget, focus on the essentials: temperature range (most budget models cover 180°F to 500°F), build gauge, hopper capacity, and warranty. You’ll give up some insulation and fancy WiFi features, but the best cheap pellet grills still turn out succulent ribs, juicy brisket, and smoky chicken. We’ve combed through owner feedback, long-term reviews, and expert test data to find the three that deliver the most bang for your buck.

Best for specific needs

Best for beginners

Newcomers to pellet smoking should prioritize ease of use and forgiving temperature control. The Green Mountain Grill Daniel Boone runs smoothly out of the box, holds its temperature so you don’t have to fuss with it, and has a large community to help troubleshoot any questions. It’s the least intimidating entry into real wood-fired barbecue. Our pick: Green Mountain Grill Daniel Boone.

Best for low-maintenance long cooks

If you love overnight brisket smokes or want to set it and forget it for 10-plus hours, hopper size and ash cleanup matter most. The Camp Chef SmokePro DLX offers the largest hopper in this group and a unique cleanout system that empties ash without a vacuum. That combo makes it the champ for marathon sessions. Our pick: Camp Chef SmokePro DLX.

Best for big parties on a tight budget

When you need to feed a crowd but your wallet is screaming, the Z Grills 700D delivers the most cooking area per dollar. You’ll get enough space to cook for a backyard party, and while you sacrifice some temperature precision and build quality, the results will still impress guests who didn’t have to tend a fire all day. Our pick: Z Grills 700D.

Our verdict

For most people, the Green Mountain Grill Daniel Boone is the clear winner: it balances performance, build, and price better than any other budget pellet smoker on the market. If your priorities lean toward marathon cooks with easy cleanup, the Camp Chef SmokePro DLX is a fantastic alternative. And if you absolutely must spend the least possible amount, the Z Grills 700D will get you smoking, just know you’re trading some reliability for that rock-bottom price.
