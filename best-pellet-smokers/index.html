Quick answer

The Traeger Pro 575 is the best pellet smoker for most people, offering reliable Wi-Fi, consistent temperature control, and a sturdy build at a reasonable price.

The Pit Boss 700FB is the top budget pick for beginners or casual smokers, while the Traeger Ironwood 885 and Green Mountain Grill Daniel Boone cater to enthusiasts seeking premium features or advanced control.

Pellet smokers have revolutionized backyard barbecue by combining the convenience of a gas grill with the wood-fired flavor of a traditional smoker. They use an auger to feed wood pellets into a burn pot, regulated by an electronic controller that maintains a set temperature – no more babysitting coals or wood chunks. The category now offers everything from Wi-Fi-enabled models that let you monitor cooks from your phone to budget-friendly grills that deliver surprisingly good smoke flavor. When choosing a pellet smoker, focus on build quality (thicker steel retains heat better), temperature range (most reach 180°F to 500°F), cooking area (adequate for your typical cook), and controller features like PID algorithms for steady temps or Wi-Fi for remote monitoring. Pellet capacity matters too – larger hoppers mean less refilling during long cooks. We considered these factors along with owner satisfaction and expert reviews to rank the best options for 2026.
